Pictures of Places in Japan

A very modest gallery (compared to the two filled photo albums I have) but I tried to give a quick sense of the different kinds of places in Japan.
KURE: IRIFUNE PARK A quiet local park with a hilltop view of Kure’s naval dockyards. Early mornings here are peaceful, with cicadas buzzing and the sounds of the port below.
KURE: PORT TOWER The red tower along the bay offers a 360-degree view of the shipyard and surrounding Seto Inland Sea. A perfect place to watch the sunset or ships come and go.
KURE: YAMATO MUSEUM Dedicated to Japan’s most famous battleship, this museum is striking and quiet inside. The massive model of the Yamato itself dominates the space. Worth a visit at least once.
KURE: ONDO NO SETO Known for its curved bridge and strong tidal currents. It’s a quiet area to walk or bike, and in the summer you’ll see people fishing off the rocks. Peaceful and timeless.
KURE: BLUE LINE COASTAL ROAD A famous biking path that hugs the coastline with views of distant islands and anchored warships. Early morning rides here are unforgettable — mist over the sea and no traffic.
KURE: HIRO BASE GATE One of the entrances to the naval base. Not touristy, but walking by here reminds you of Kure’s past and present as a military port town. Guards wave if you pass daily.
KURE: REKISHI NO MICHI A walking path with markers detailing Kure’s history. Not long, but thoughtfully maintained. At night, it’s lit with soft lamps — a quiet path through local memory.
KURE: MOUNT YASUMIYAMA One of the higher vantage points in Kure. From the top, the view includes the shipyards, bridges, and distant Hiroshima Bay. Hike early and catch the morning fog rising.
KURE: KIRIKUSHI SHRINE Tucked away in the hills. Stone steps, moss-covered gates, and old trees make this place feel untouched. In spring, the cherry blossoms frame the shrine beautifully.
KURE: CENTRAL ARCADE The main shopping street — part Showa, part modern. It’s a mix of old shops, bakeries, arcades, and shuttered storefronts. I walk it often on errands or just to think.
